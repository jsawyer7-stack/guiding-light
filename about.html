Who We Are

A church that keeps showing up.

Guiding Light Apostolic Church of Christ is a Christ-centered, Bible-believing, Spirit-filled congregation in Camden, North Carolina. For more than six decades, we've existed for one reason: to know Jesus and to make Him known.

We believe the local church is a family — not a building, not a program, not a Sunday performance. It's where broken people find healing, where seekers find answers, where the weary find rest, and where ordinary believers are sent out to shine the light of Christ into every corner of Camden County.

Leadership

Meet Pastor Spellman.

A shepherd's heart, three decades of ministry, and a relentless love for the people of Camden County.

"I'm eager to help you no matter where you are in your walk with God. The door's open, the light's on, and there's a seat saved for you."

Pastor Daniel L. Spellman has served Guiding Light as Senior Pastor since February 2010. Ordained into ministry in 1994 and called to the eldership in 2001, his twenty-plus years of pastoral experience are matched by a deep personal faith and a commitment to the apostolic doctrine that has anchored this congregation for generations.

He and his wife Tonya have raised six children in this congregation — a reminder that family is not a side-program at Guiding Light, it's the center. He preaches like a man who has walked what he teaches, and pastors like a friend you've known for years.

Our Story

Six decades.
One faithful God.

From a small Camden gathering in the 1960s to the thriving congregation we are today — the Lord has been faithful every step of the way.

1964 · The Beginning

A light is lit in Camden County

Guiding Light is founded by a small band of believers committed to apostolic doctrine and the Acts 2:38 gospel. The first services are held with just a handful of families and a single conviction: the Word is enough.

1970s · Roots Deepen

A permanent home on Ivy Neck Road

A permanent sanctuary is built. Sunday school begins. The church starts sending ministry teams to serve surrounding communities.

1994 · A Future Pastor Called

Daniel Spellman is ordained

A future pastor begins his ministry journey — serving first as a youth leader, then as an elder, growing into the calling God would make clear over the decades.

2010 · A New Season

Pastor Spellman installed as Senior Pastor

Under his shepherding the church leans into deeper teaching, expanded outreach, and a renewed vision for the next generation.

Today · Still Shining

Still sending, still saving seats

Weekly worship, midweek Bible study, vibrant youth and Sunday school ministries, and a growing online reach bring the light of Christ to families across Camden County and beyond.

What We Believe

Rooted in Scripture.

These aren't just doctrines we recite — they're truths we build our lives on.

01 / The Bible

The Word is our foundation.

We believe the Scriptures of the Old and New Testaments are the inspired, infallible Word of God — sufficient for faith, practice, and salvation.

2 Timothy 3:16
02 / One God

There is one God.

We believe in one God — revealed as Father in creation, as the Son for redemption, and as the Holy Ghost dwelling in the believer.

Deuteronomy 6:4
03 / Jesus Christ

Jesus is Lord.

We believe Jesus Christ — born of a virgin, crucified, buried, and risen — is the Savior of the world and the only name by which we must be saved.

Acts 4:12
04 / Salvation

Repent. Believe. Follow.

We believe salvation comes by God's grace through repentance, baptism in the name of Jesus Christ, and the indwelling of the Holy Ghost.

Acts 2:38
05 / The Holy Spirit

Spirit-filled living.

We believe the Holy Ghost empowers, comforts, convicts, and sanctifies the believer — producing the fruit of the Spirit and the gifts for ministry.

Galatians 5:22
06 / The Church

We are family.

We believe the Church is the body of Christ — every believer called to love, serve, witness, and build one another up until He returns.

Ephesians 4:16
07 / Christian Living

Walk in the light.

We believe a changed heart produces a changed life — holiness inside and out, love for God and love for neighbor, the world seeing Christ through us.

Matthew 5:16
08 / His Return

He is coming again.

We believe in the personal, bodily return of Jesus Christ — the resurrection of the saints, and eternity with Him. Come quickly, Lord Jesus.

1 Thessalonians 4:16

Core Values

How we live it out.

Four commitments that shape every service, every ministry, every handshake at the door.

Value 01

Bible first.

We refuse to trade the clarity of Scripture for the trend of the moment. Every message, every class, every counseling conversation starts with the Word.

Value 02

Family always.

Church is where names are remembered, where children are celebrated, where widows and singles and seniors are seen — because a family doesn't leave anyone at the door.

Value 03

Prayer always.

Before the sermon, after the altar call, in the hospital, over the meal — we pray. Not as a ritual, but because we believe heaven actually listens.

Value 04

Outward facing.

A church that turns inward starves. We're called to the lost, to the hungry, to the hurting — in Camden County and wherever the Spirit leads.
